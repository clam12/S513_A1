Tim Duncan

About Him

Timothy Theodore Duncan, born on April 25, 1976 in Christiansted, U.S. Virgin Islands. Duncan would play his college basketball at Wake Forest and upon graduating, he would enter the NBA Draft in 1997. On June 25, 1997, Duncan would be drafted 1st overall by the San Antonio Spurs where he would play his entire legendary career at for 19 seasons. It can be argued that Duncan is one of the greatest power forwards to play the game of basketball. Throughout his lusterous career, Duncan would total over 25,000 points, 15,000 rebounds and 4,000 assists and would go on to win 5 NBA Championships, all with the San Antonio Spurs. On July 11, 2016, Duncan would announce his retirement from the NBA and would later have his jersey retired by the Spurs on December 18, 2016.

Why He's My Favourite

Just like hockey, I've been watching basketball for a long time but not as long as I have watched hockey. With Duncan, its not so much as his on court play which I admire most about him but its more so with his off court persona. When you hear other players and coaches talk about Duncan, they never have anything bad to say about him. He has also done a lot for the community of San Antonio from establishingthe Tim Duncan Foundation which serves the areas of health awareness, educataion and youth sports to also supporting Children's Bereavement Center, Children Center's of San Antonio and the CancerTherapy and Research Center. Also, three of the most important values to Duncan I find very inspiring. These three values are dedication, teamwork and camaraderie.
